Who is "ななひら(Nanahira)"?

  • Age: 34 Years Old (34 Years, 8 Months)
  • Born: August 31st 1991
  • Gender: Female
  • Status: Active
  • Years Active: 2008 - Present Day

Nanahira (ななひら) is an "Utaite" mainly known for her sweet, sassy and youthful-like voice which has been described over the years as very "Loli"-like. However, as of recent years, she has started to use more varieties in her singing styles across various Albums and Song Covers. Ranging from her iconic "Loli"-like style to a style similar to that of a Mature and "Bijin"-like voice, which showcases not only her talent at different vocal ranges for differing song styles and covers but also years of experience in her field of work.

She is often associated with Artists such as LamazeP and Camellia, as their songs fit her voice very well. This is most notably seen in a specific game genre known as "Rhythm Games", where her collaboration works with said artists can be played as a "Beatmap" in said game's styling. One such "Rhythm Game"" that features her works is Muse Dash. However, she has also collaborated with quite a number of different Utaite, ranging from well-known names of Reol, Soraru, and 96Neko, to lesser-known names such as Kano, mega, Kokon Koko, Momobako and more recently, Wotoha.
In more recent years, specifically from March 9th, 2020 onwards, Nanahira debuted as a "Virtual Youtuber" and has been streaming on her Youtube Channel, ななひら / Nanahira, albeit only occasionally. This is also the Youtube Channel to which she uploads Covers, Originals and Albums she has done before. She also occassionally acts as a Music Producer, especially when producing songs with Nana Haruka, an "UTAU" who are voice sampled by herself.

Her Official Website can be found at nanahira.jp.

How I personally found ななひら(Nanahira)

Being a fan of "Vocaloid", EDM, "Japanese FuturePop", "FutureBass" styled music, alongside many other genres, I naturally gravitated towards mainly Japanese Artists such as Moe Shop, Ujico*/Snail's House etc, that did these styles of Music and upload it to Youtube. These songs are then sometimes featured on "Rhythm Games" as a playable song after the developer collaborates with said Artist to use their song in their game. One of these games, being Muse Dash in particular, had songs from those artists featured in it, but also had songs from Nanahira and her collaboration works.
It was through this game, that I came to find out about her, and grow to love her work even more outside of the Game itself. She very much tailors heavily to the type of music I'm into now as a person, but also pays "tribute" to a more nostalgic side of music tastes growing up due to the Music Genre's origination in Japanese Culture and stylings. I mainly grew up with alot of heavy influence from various forms of Japanese Media, be it Anime or various JRPG Games such as The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, Final Fantasy: X etc, that helped shape my music tastes today and why I'm into such genres of music and music artists.

Word Meanings

Utaite - Utaite (歌い手) is a Japanese term for people who cover previously released songs and post them on Nico Nico Douga and YouTube under the utattemita category. The term "utaite" is unique to Nico Nico Douga singers, making it different from Kashu (歌手), which means "singer" in general. Generally, utaite do cover songs for VOCALOID original songs, anime, game-related songs, and J-Pop. They also do parodies with different lyrics for popular songs, intended for amusement. Most utaite exclusively cover songs and upload under the utattemita section, but some of them have doujin circles and release original music/drama CDs during events like Comic Market and VOCALOID M@ster, frequently referred to as Comiket and VoMas respectively.
Source: Here

Will not personally explain what exactly a Loli is due to the word itself having a very negative stigma behind it. Just know that it's often used to either refer to a person "Youthful" in some way or form, whether apperance wise or personality etc. It's very open to interpretation.

Bijin - Bijin (美人) directly translates to "beautiful person". However, in Japanese, this is mostly used for Women due to the first Kanji character, Bi (美), having a feminine connotation.
Definition sourced from Wikipedia and Reddit.

Rhythm Game - A Genre of video games that focuses on using various Music and a specific set of buttons/inputs from the player to challenge their sense of rhythm while playing the song's beatmap.
Sourced from my own personal definition after looking through Google and personal experience.

Beatmap - Often used to describe the layout of various inputs the player would need to do in order to keep in rhythm with the song in question. The term is used often in games like Osu and Beat Saber.
Personal Definition

Virtual Youtuber - A Virtual Youtuber, or often referred to as a "VTuber", is someone who is an online entertainer that uses a virtually made Avatar in order to maintain an anonymous online persona seperate from their Real Life counterpart. The Avatar is often times animated via Real-Time motion capture via Phone to capture movement and facial expressions. The Avatars are often times designed with Popular Japanese Culture and aesthetics, such as Anime and Manga.
The trend originated in Japan originally as a gateway into "Anonymous Idolship", where the person can be an "Idol" in a digital form without risking compromising their Real Life safety like traditional Idols do. Nowadays, most VTubers act as live streamers on platforms such as Twitch or Youtube for their Audience, with some even being professional singers/music producers despite their Real Life counterpart being completely detatched from their online persona. They are also often under a specializd agency to help promote them but some "VTubers" are classified under what is known as "Indie VTubers", where they do not fall under any specific Corporation and are free to act and take part in any form of entertainment they wish, however these "Indie VTubers" are self-funded via their own Resources and fan donations rather than being backed by a Corporation.
One extremely popular example of a "VTuber" corporation is Hololive Production.
Sourced via Wikipedia and personal Knowledge on the topic.

UTAU - UTAU is a Japanese singing synthesizer application created by Ameya/Ayame (飴屋/菖蒲). This program is similar to the "Vocaloid" software, with the difference being it is shareware instead of under a third party licensing. It allows users to create singing vocals by inputting melodies and lyrics. Community voicebanks are referred to as "UTAUloids", with a famous example being Kasane Teto, who posed as a real "Vocaloid" as part of an April Fool's joke in 2008 and made UTAU itself famous for the first time.
Source: Here

Vocaloid - Similar to UTAU, Vocaloid is a voice synthesizer application that enables users to synthesize "singing" via typing in lyrics, melody and also speech in the script of the required words. The Software was originally only available in English and Japanese, but added support for other language "Vocaloids" later on via Vocaloid 3. An extremely famous example of a "Vocaloid" would be Hatsune Miku.
Sourced via Wikipedia information and interpreted in my own way.

Japanese FuturePop - A "J-Pop" sub-genre of music that distinguishes itself from "Western Futurepop" by reflecting high energy "J-Pop" and merges "Idol"-style like melodies with futuristic EDM, Electropop and Future Bass genres. The sub-genre originated from evolving out of "Shibuya-kei" styled music. Has an extremely diverse category of songs that fall under this sub-genre.
Sourced via googling since no one site had the same exact definition.

FutureBass - A sub-genre of EDM that blends together the Trap and Dubstep styled music with warmer, melodic chord progression. It's mainly notable for it's "Dream-like" synth drops and vibrant sound design.
Sourced via Googling and information from Wikipedia mainly.
